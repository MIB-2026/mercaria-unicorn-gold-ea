1. About the Expert Advisor

Mercaria Unicorn is an adaptive grid trading robot for MetaTrader 5, developed specifically for Gold (XAUUSD), but applicable to any CFD instrument by adjusting the grid step. The main difference from typical "fixed-parameter grids" is that the EA automatically adjusts the number of levels and lot size to match your deposit and chosen market model, rather than trading "blindly" based on a single pre-configured situation.

2. Quick Start in 4 Steps

Before the first launch: test the EA on a demo account for at least 1–2 trading sessions. Grid strategies are highly sensitive to broker conditions (spread, commission, swap), and the same settings can yield different results on different accounts.

Step 1. Installation from MQL5 Market

  1. After purchasing on www.mql5.com/en/market, the EA automatically becomes available in your MT5 terminal — no need to download and copy files manually. Make sure you are logged into your terminal with the same MQL5 account used for the purchase.
  2. In MetaTrader 5, open the Navigator panel (Ctrl+N), expand the Market section, and you will see Mercaria Unicorn in the list of purchased products.
  3. If the EA is not listed: go to Toolbox → Market tab → Purchases, select Mercaria Unicorn, and click Install. If the terminal asks to activate, follow the prompts (each MQL5 Market purchase provides 5 activations).
  4. Open the chart of the instrument you want to trade (XAUUSD is recommended, any timeframe — the logic does not depend on the chart's TF).
  5. Drag the EA from Navigator → Market onto the chart. The settings window (F7) will open.
Demo Account: A Market purchase works on MT5 demo accounts using the same activations. Test the EA on a demo account for 1–2 sessions before going live.

When you drag the EA onto the chart, the settings window (F7) will open. For your first run, we recommend leaving most parameters at their default values and adjusting only these key inputs:

When the EA opens the first trade: the status will change to ACTIVE +1 (in profit) or ACTIVE -1 (DD) (in drawdown), and the GRID section will show active grid parameters.

An additional layer of profit protection. It triggers earlier than the Pyramid Trail once the peak profit reaches a minimum threshold. This is a key safety feature.

ParameterDefault ValueDescription
UseProfitProtecttrueEnable percentage-based profit protection.
ProfitProtectMinUSD5.0Minimum peak profit in USD required to activate protection. Prior to this, the protection remains inactive. Scales automatically with lot size (see §4.6).

Example: The EA opens a grid and profit reaches +12 USD (peak). With ProfitProtectDropPct = 30, the allowed pullback is 30% of 12 = 3.6 USD. Once profit drops to +8.4 USD, the pool closes, securing +8.4 USD. Without this protection, the trade could have retraced back into a drawdown.

4.6. Profit-to-SL Bonus — Cushion from Closed Profit v3.572

An optional feature: a percentage of each profitable closed pool is added to the pool's allowed drawdown as a "cushion". The more you earn in a session, the larger the buffer for handling the next market move. The cushion is reset on an SL hit. This is useful for aggressive scaling, as it leverages accumulated profit rather than risking the initial capital.

ParameterDefault ValueDescription
UseProfitToSLBonusfalseEnable the bonus cushion. Disabled by default as a standard static SL is easier to manage for most users.
ProfitToSLPercent50.0Percentage of closed profit allocated to the SL cushion. E.g., 50% of a $10 profit pool adds $5 to the cushion.
ProfitToSLCapUSD0.0Maximum limit for the accumulated cushion in USD. 0 = unlimited. If set to 200, the cushion will not expand the SL by more than $200.
Example: Base MaxDrawdownUSD=100, UseProfitToSLBonus=true, ProfitToSLPercent=50:
  1. Pool #1 closes with +$10 → cushion = +$5. Effective SL = −$105.
  2. Pool #2 closes with +$8 → cushion = +$9. Effective SL = −$109.
  3. Pool #3 closes with +$12 → cushion = +$15. Effective SL = −$115.
  4. Pool #4 hits SL at $115 → cushion is reset to 0. Effective SL returns to −$100.
In the panel → RISK section → the SL buffer line shows the current cushion and effective SL.
Recommended for experienced traders who want to "reinvest" a portion of profits into risk protection.

4.12. Balance, Leverage, and Account Types — Key Considerations

Depending on your broker and account type, identical EA parameters can result in different margin requirements and limitations. Three main factors play a key role: account balance, leverage, and account type (Standard / ECN / Cent / Hedging / Netting). They directly determine how many grid levels can fit within your margin and what lot size is safe.

Impact of Leverage on Margin Requirements

Margin required to hold 0.01 lot of XAUUSD ≈ Price × ContractSize × Lots / Leverage. For gold ContractSize = 100, so at a price of $4500 with a 0.01 lot: 4500 × 100 × 0.01 / Leverage = 4500 / Leverage (e.g. $9 per level at 1:500). Higher leverage reduces the margin occupied by each grid level, allowing more levels to fit within your account deposit.

5. Usage Scenarios

Mercaria Unicorn is a flexible tool. It can be run fully automated, semi-automated with manual profile switching, or purely as a manual position manager with automatic trailing. Below are three workflow setups with concrete numbers for typical deposits.

5.1. Fully Automated Setup — For Hands-Off Trading

Best for: Traders who want a "set-and-forget" robot. This is the starting scenario for most users.

Configuration

  4. UseProfitProtect = true, ProfitProtectDropPct = 25 (aggressive profit locking).
  5. TargetProfitUSD = 2–3% of deposit (the EA automatically stops trading once this target is reached).
  6. UseLotScaling = true — all thresholds scale with the active lot size.

5.2. Semi-Automated Setup — Volatility Profiling via News Calendar

Best for: Intermediate traders who track news events (via economic calendars like investing.com) and understand the impact of CPI, FOMC, and NFP releases.

Methodology

Evaluate the economic calendar before each trading session and select an appropriate volatility profile (NORMAL / SPIKE_M / SPIKE_L / BLACK SWAN). Switch the profile via the panel with a single click, and the EA will immediately adjust its sizing calculations.

Daily Workflow

  1. Preparation (7:00–9:00 GMT): Check the news calendar:
    • Empty calendar / minor events → start on NORMAL.
    • CPI / FOMC / ECB release scheduled → start on NORMAL, switch to SPIKE_M 30–60 minutes before the release.
    • NFP or major unscheduled central bank announcements → start on SPIKE_L.
    • Geopolitical crisis / market shock → use BLACK SWAN.
  2. AutoSizing_Mode = FULL — the EA fully manages the N×lot configuration.

  4. Set the daily session target (TargetProfitUSD) to 3–5% of the deposit. Once reached, the EA halts trading until the next day.

5.3. Manual Entry Setup — For Professional Position Management

Best for: Experienced traders who have their own entry signals (price action, key support/resistance levels, order flow) and want to use the EA strictly for automated trade management and recovery.

Methodology

You identify the setup and execute the trade. The EA:

  • Does not open initial trades automatically (set to OFF).
  • Once you click BUY or SELL on the panel, it immediately takes over management:
    • If the trade moves against you, it adds grid recovery levels.
    • If the trade moves in your favor, it trails the profit and applies percentage-based protection.
    • Closes the entire pool automatically at TP, SL, or trailing exit.

Configuration

  1. Click the OFF button on the panel to disable automatic entry signals.
  2. Set AutoSizing_Mode = OFF so the EA does not alter your custom parameters.
  3. Configure LotSize, MaxDrawdownUSD, PyramidTrailStart/Step, and ProfitProtectMinUSD/DropPct to fit your manual system.
  4. When your entry signal triggers, click BUY or SELL on the panel to open the initial position.
  5. The EA will manage the position to completion. You can exit manually at any time using X CLOSE (requires confirmation) or PANIC (instant execution).

6. Tips & Tricks

Run 2 Demo Sessions Before Live Trading

Spend one session on NORMAL and another on SPIKE_M during news events. Record average pool duration, win rate, and drawdown in the STATS section to establish your baseline.

Use SIZING as a Risk Calculator

In ADVISORY mode, the "Recommended setups" table displays deposit requirements for each profile, allowing you to quickly assess risk compatibility.

Monitor the Margin Call Bar

If the bar turns orange or red, your active sizing is too aggressive for the current market movement. Consider lowering your lot size or switching to a safer profile (SPIKE_L / BSW).

Check "Recent max move" in STATS

This metric shows the largest price swings recorded during recent grid cycles. If this value approaches the limits of your active profile, switch to a wider profile.

Use LOCK Mode during distractions

If you need to leave your desk or attend a call, lock the panel. Grids and risk protection will continue running, but accidental clicks are blocked.

Unique Magic Numbers

If running multiple copies of the EA on the same account, assign a unique InputMagic to each copy to prevent them from reading each other's trades.

7. FAQ

What is the minimum required deposit?

The absolute minimum is $300 using a LotSize=0.01 on the NORMAL profile. A comfortable minimum for standard brokers is $500–$1,000. The exact requirements for each profile are displayed in the SIZING → "Recommended setups" panel section. See the table in §5.1 for sample configurations across various account balances.

What is new in v3.572?

  • Profit-to-SL Bonus (§4.6) — optional drawdown cushion funded by closed profits.
  • Lot Scaling (§4.7) — TP, SL, trailing, and protection thresholds scale dynamically with lot size.
  • VirtualBalanceUSD — fixed virtual balance for AutoSizing calculations.
  • Session auto-restart — clicking ON after reaching a session target automatically starts a new session.
  • Technical improvements — improved Session PnL accuracy and stability on EA restart/removal; refined input-parameter interface.

Which instruments can be traded?

The EA is optimized for XAUUSD. It can be applied to other CFDs by adjusting GridStepPoints to match the volatility of the asset. We do not recommend trading cryptocurrencies without thorough backtesting due to extreme baseline volatility.

What timeframe should I use?

Any timeframe. The EA's logic operates independently of the chart timeframe. M5 or M15 are recommended for convenient visual monitoring.

What does the RESET button do?

Resets the current trading session: clears Closed PnL, Pools closed, Win rate, Streak, Max drawdown, and sets the current account balance as the new session baseline. **It does not close active trades** (use X CLOSE or PANIC). Use this to start a fresh trading session (e.g. at the start of a trading day or after hitting a daily goal).

What happens if I click ON after reaching the session profit target?

The EA detects that the session concluded via TargetProfitUSD (or the MaxLossCount limit). Clicking ON initiates a new session: it resets session statistics and activates the profit target goal again.

Can I switch sizing modes or profiles while a grid is active?

Yes. The active grid will be managed using the parameters it was opened with. Changes will apply to the **next** grid pool. Switching settings on the fly is completely safe.

What does "AUTO OFF" mean?

Auto trading is disabled. Active positions will still be managed to completion (TP/SL/trail/protection), but no new grid cycles will be initiated. This allows for a graceful exit from the market.

What is the difference between PANIC and X CLOSE?

PANIC closes all trades instantly with a single click. X CLOSE requires a double click (shows a "CONFIRM?" confirmation step). PANIC is faster but carries a risk of accidental trigger.

What is Lot Scaling and how does it work?

When the EA trades with a lot size different from the reference lot (BaseLotReference, default 0.01), all USD-based thresholds (TP, SL, trailing, profit protection) are multiplied by the ratio: active_lot / BaseLotReference. This ensures that the risk profile scales proportionally with your account balance. TargetProfitUSD is excluded from scaling and remains absolute.

What is VirtualBalanceUSD?

A simulated balance input for AutoSizing. If set to 0, the real account balance is used. If set to a specific value (e.g., 1000), the sizing calculations simulate a $1,000 account. This is useful for copying settings across different accounts or running conservative tests. When active, "(virt)" appears next to the balance in the SIZING section.

Are my control panel preferences saved?

Yes. Panel coordinates, LOCK / READ ONLY states, and collapsed section choices are stored in MT5 Global Variables and persist across restarts.

What does "cooldown" mean?

A brief technical pause of a few seconds after a grid closes. It ensures the broker processes all closures fully before the EA scans for a new entry signal, preventing overlapping orders.

How do I update the EA to a new version?

Update notifications are handled by MetaTrader. Go to Toolbox → Market → Purchases → Mercaria Unicorn → Update. Settings and active states are preserved during updates.

What does "Distance to MC" represent?

The distance in points from the current price to your broker's margin call limit. Color cues: green = safe (over 500 points), orange = warning (200–500 points), red = critical (less than 200 points). If red, consider reducing volume or switching to a safer profile.
